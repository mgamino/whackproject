Beebe Hall Profile:

We wanted to create a web resource to help incoming and returning students find the most comfortable and accessible home in one of the campus dorms. We know that “accessible” means different things to different people, so instead of just breaking down buildings into broad categories of “accessible” or “not accessible,” we’ve provided information about specific traits and amenities of the buildings so that the students themselves can decide what is accessible for them.

Mobility OUT: Approx. walk time from Nearest dhall Pomeroy - across the quad. Library Moderate walk, but down and up hills. Campus center Relatively close, but down a hill. Gradation & stuff of hills Beebe has two main approaches. One is a short & fairly steep (but smoothly paved) path that leads to a few steps at the building entrance. The other approach is a longer but less steep paved path that leads to an entrance with a wheelchair ramp. IN: Mobility Elevator or nah Beebe has a fairly narrow elevator Steps, number of steps One entrance of Beebe has several steps before the door. In the basement, there’s TK steps up from where the elevator stops in order to access the laundry room. Doorknob types Beebe has TK style doorknobs. Amenities Laundry Beebe has laundry in the basement, but students must go up and down a few steps in order to access the room. Restrooms The first floor as an accessible restroom. Kitchen Beebe has a kitchen on each floor. On some floors the kitchen is divided, but on others it is just one room. Sound/Alarms Fire alarms - flashing lights (maybe in some rooms?) Super loud heaters Environment Allergens (carpeting vs hardwood, mix of both, etc) heating/cooling (no AC, heating in every room)
